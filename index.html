What - Why - How Analysis

What (Data)

Monthly average temperature records from 2015 to 2024 across 12 UK regions, showing seasonal variations and regional differences.

Why (Task)

Enable comparison of temperature patterns across years and regions, identify climate trends, and detect anomalous seasons.

How (Idiom)

· Consider using a line chart instead of a bar chart, with one line for each year and using fewer colors to make the trend clearer · Reduce the number of data points appropriately, and only show data points for January and July each year to highlight seasonal changes · Add trend lines, such as a 10-year rolling average, to help viewers understand long-term climate change trends

What - Why - How Analysis

What (Data)

Superstore sales data, including sales, profits, customer types, and year-over-year comparisons.

Why (Task)

Provide actionable insights for performance optimization.

How (Idiom)

"Less is more" → Remove unnecessary borders, gridlines, and redundant labels.

"Show, don’t tell" → Use colors and direct labeling instead of legends.

"Consistency is key" → Standardize fonts, colors, and alignment.

"Guide the eye" → Use white space and structured alignment to enhance navigation.

"Function over form" → Prioritize readability and usability over decorative elements.

"Emphasize what matters" → Use color and bold formatting selectively to highlight key data points.
